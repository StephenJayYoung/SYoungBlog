How to Become a Good Developer

-Once you make a decision, the universe conspires to make it happen -Emerson

Most people want to be good at the things they do. One of the most challenging aspects of this concept is figuring out how to obtain that “goodness” level. I want to become a better coder. I’ve taken a number of different approaches at reaching this elusive adjective, and this post is another crack at motivating myself to get there.

Here are a few of the things that I’ve done to try to get good at coding:

  1. Spent a month learning HTML, CSS, and Git through Treehouse videos.
  2. Enrolled in Portland Code School’s Javascript Immersion Course.
  3. Coded every day for 255 days and counting..
  4. Went to Meet-Ups and talked with people.
  5. Studied books (Javascript: The Good Parts, Bootstrap Book, Ducket Book)
  6. Made things: www.thejbac.com, www.IsabellaYoga.com. www.YoungCreativeSpace.com, www.getcityhacker.com, among others.
  7. Tracked my time, and committed to coding at least 20 hours per week, if not more.
  8. Did freelancing work for real clients.

These things have all helped me to get where I am in my coding journey, but recently, I realized that learning from other people in a structured classroom setting helps to motivate me, undertake projects that get me out of my comfort zone, and enables me to meet more cool people in the Portland tech community.

So, in May, I enrolled in Portland Code School’s Advanced Front-End Development Course.

This course entails two night classes per week over the course of four weeks. It’s structured so that we get out of it what we put into it, and due to that, we’ll be covering a lot of material.

Here is a brief outline of some of the things that we’ll be going over:

  1. Ajax
  2. Preprocessors like SASS and Stylus .
  3. JQuery
  4. JavaScript
  5. Going deeper into HTML and CSS
  6. More bootstrap!
  7. Less
  8. Package manager’s like Yeoman
  9. Gulp
  10. API's
  11. Browser-side storage
  12. Going Deeper into Chrome Developer Tools
  13. Selenium Browser testing
  14. Tuts Plus/Codecademy tutorials

I don’t think that I’ll be able to be incredible at all of these things over the course of a month, but I do know that I’ll be able to build a solid foundation over this time period. I can then use this to propel me to go deeper into the aforementioned tools, thereby continuing my coding journey. Thus, the plan going forward is to create Github Repos for becoming good at some of the above-mentioned tools, and to talk about them here, in a living, breathing blogpost. Here goes nuthin…
